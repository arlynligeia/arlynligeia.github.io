Short Film

EL INFIERNO DE OZUL

EL INFIERNO DE OZUL is a silent short film written, directed, shot, starred in, and edited by Arlyn Ligeia. Created as part of an academic exam during her ongoing studies in Audiovisual Communication, the film pays tribute to both silent cinema and gothic film, blending classic references with a raw and deeply personal vision.

With very limited resources and facing financial hardship, Ligeia was forced to improvise, but turned those constraints into creativity. This short stands as a testament to her belief in art as a means of survival.

Music Video Direction

BLUE - DESPERTAR

DESPERTAR this was Ligeia’s first audiovisual creation, made for the Blue Band of Moquegua. The music video was produced in 2021, during the height of the pandemic. For its production, Ligeia chose three locations: two in the city where she lived and one in Lima.

Due to the health restrictions imposed by COVID-19, Ligeia independently managed the entire production. She contacted everyone who could contribute to the project, arranged the locations by phone, and coordinated every detail. She directed the entire storyline set in Lima remotely via video calls. For the scenes filmed in her own city, she was present in person, handling both directing and filming herself.

She also edited the entire music video on her own.

The video explores Gothic Romanticism as its central theme, delving into fantasy and deep emotions such as solitude, presented through a melancholic and evocative aesthetic.
